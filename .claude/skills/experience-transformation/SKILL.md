# Experience Transformation & Design — Service-Specific Reasoning

## What This Service Changes

Experience Transformation redesigns specific **customer journeys** — the end-to-end
flows a customer goes through when interacting with the company (e.g., checkout,
onboarding, returns, account management, booking). The engagement identifies the
highest-impact journeys, maps friction points and "moments that matter," then
redesigns the experience to improve conversion, retention, and efficiency.

This is NOT a technology project. It's a strategy and design engagement that produces
blueprints, governance models, and prioritized roadmaps. Technology changes may follow
but are not part of the engagement scope.

## Scoping the Addressable Base for ET

**Addressable base = revenue flowing through the specific journey(s) being redesigned.**

The engagement tier determines how many journeys are in scope:

| Tier | Journeys | Duration | Price | Addressable Base |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| CORE | 1 priority journey | 8-10 weeks | $150-200K | Revenue through that ONE journey (e.g., mobile checkout = $X of digital revenue) |
| EXPANDED | 2-3 priority journeys | 12-16 weeks | $275-350K | Combined revenue through those journeys |
| ENTERPRISE | Full customer lifecycle | 16-20+ weeks | $400-500K+ | Broader revenue base, but scope to the specific lifecycle stages being redesigned |

**How to estimate journey-level revenue:**
1. Start with total digital/online revenue (from filings)
2. Identify which journey the engagement targets (checkout, onboarding, renewal, etc.)
3. Estimate what % of digital revenue flows through that journey
   - Checkout/purchase flow: typically 100% of digital revenue passes through it, but the
     IMPROVEMENT opportunity is on the conversion gap (abandonment rate)
   - Onboarding/activation: affects downstream retention, not direct revenue
   - Support/service: affects cost base, not revenue
4. For conversion-focused journeys, the addressable base is:
   `digital_revenue / current_conversion_rate * improvement_potential`
   or more simply: `digital_revenue * lift_percentage`

**Common scoping mistakes for ET:**
- Using total company revenue (includes wholesale, physical stores, B2B — none touched by a digital journey redesign)
- Using total digital revenue when the engagement only touches one journey
- Treating a CORE engagement as if it covers the full customer lifecycle

## Enterprise-Level Indicators

These are the value creation categories that ET impacts. Use them to frame your
narrative and identify which KPIs matter most.

### Retention & Loyalty
- Customer retention rate
- Repeat engagement / repeat purchase
- Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)
- NPS / loyalty trend

**Financial mechanism:** Reducing churn preserves revenue. A 1pp reduction in churn
for a company with 100K customers at $500/year = $500K preserved revenue annually.

### Revenue Performance
- Conversion at priority journey stages
- Revenue at risk reduction
- Revenue per customer

**Financial mechanism:** Improving conversion or AOV at key journey stages directly
increases revenue flowing through those stages.

### Margin & Efficiency
- Cost-to-serve per customer
- Service volume and resolution time
- Process duplication reduction

**Financial mechanism:** Better self-service UX reduces support contacts. Streamlined
journeys reduce operational complexity. These are cost savings, not revenue gains.

### Execution Discipline (qualitative — do NOT model financially)
- Speed from insight to initiative rollout
- Clear ownership of priority journeys
- Cross-functional alignment on roadmap

**Note:** These are real outcomes of ET engagements but cannot be quantified in dollars.
Mention them qualitatively in the narrative: "Beyond the financial impact, this
engagement would improve cross-functional alignment on priority customer journeys."

## Sector Lenses

When you know the company's industry, use the matching lens to sharpen your reasoning.

### Retail / E-commerce
- **Revenue levers:** Purchase frequency, AOV lift, digital+in-store conversion, churn reduction (~20-25% industry benchmark for improvement)
- **Margin levers:** Lower return rates, reduced support volume, improved inventory alignment
- **Operational:** Unified digital+store execution, reduced duplication across merchandising & CX
- **What to look for:** Cart abandonment rate, mobile vs desktop conversion gap, return rate, repeat purchase rate

### Travel & Hospitality
- **Revenue levers:** Repeat bookings, occupancy rate, RevPAR, ancillary revenue (upgrades, add-ons)
- **Loyalty:** Enrollment & active usage, reduced cancellation/defection, share of wallet per guest
- **Margin:** Lower service recovery costs, reduced complaint volume, improved staffing alignment
- **Key insight:** "Less about transaction conversion, more about brand promise delivery." The guest experience across booking→arrival→stay→post-stay is the journey. Focus on promise gaps.
- **What to look for:** Loyalty program participation rate, direct vs OTA booking ratio, guest satisfaction scores, cancellation rate

### Restaurants (QSR & Full Service)
- **Revenue levers:** Visit frequency, avg ticket size, digital ordering adoption, loyalty program participation
- **Retention:** Reduced churn, guest satisfaction at peak hours, reduced brand switching
- **Margin:** Lower labor inefficiencies at peaks, reduced order errors/remakes, improved throughput & table turn times
- **Key insight:** "Less about broad transaction conversion, more about operational integration." The digital ordering→kitchen ops→service delivery chain is the journey.
- **What to look for:** Digital ordering penetration, order accuracy rate, peak hour throughput, loyalty program enrollment

### Other Industries
If no sector lens matches, fall back to the enterprise-level indicators and reason
from first principles about which customer journeys drive the most revenue or cost.

## Maturity Assessment for ET

When assessing a company's digital experience maturity (Step 4 of the framework),
focus on these ET-specific signals:

**Digital experience quality:**
- App store ratings (< 4.0 = opportunity, > 4.5 = less room)
- Website performance scores (Core Web Vitals, Lighthouse)
- Known UX issues, redesigns in progress, or recent design awards
- Competitor comparison (is their experience above or below peers?)

**Journey complexity:**
- How many distinct customer journeys exist? (More = more opportunity for a diagnostic)
- Are journeys siloed across teams? (Organizational fragmentation = ET value)
- Is there a single owner for end-to-end experience? (If not, ET provides this)

**Recent CX investments:**
- Have they recently invested in CX/UX? (Diminishing returns)
- Is this greenfield? (Larger opportunity but also more uncertainty)
- Are they in the middle of a platform migration? (Timing matters)

## Service Tier Deliverables (for narrative framing)

When writing the narrative, reference what the engagement actually delivers:

**CORE deliverables:** Discovery scan (6 exec interviews), journey & system mapping
(1 priority journey), opportunity identification ("moments that matter"), executive
alignment workshop. Optional: Spotlight Validation Sprint (quant n=300-500).

**EXPANDED deliverables:** Everything in CORE plus behavioral & segment validation
(qual + quant n=400-800), expanded blueprinting (2 priority journeys), governance &
roadmap, financial impact framing.

**ENTERPRISE deliverables:** Everything in EXPANDED plus ecosystem-level service design
(e2e lifecycle, omnichannel blueprint), operating model design (cross-functional
collaboration, AI authority thresholds), portfolio-level prioritization, change &
capability embed (change management, KPI dashboard framework).
